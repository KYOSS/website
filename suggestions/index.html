# Repositories

  • Portland Pattern Repository, the Wiki Wiki Webopen in new window the first wiki, originally in support of the then new agile methodology, it expanded to many topics of interest with more than a decade of various programmers swinging by to add their two cents to the various topics found within
  • The Jargon Fileopen in new window an early piece of work that ended up in esr's hands by the early 90s. some have claimed he 'unix-ized' it from its LISP origins, and may have wholesale created a term or two, but it remains interesting as an early source of programmer lexicon
  • textfiles dot comopen in new window if you missed your change to peruse the BBS sites of the 80s, this has a great repository of the kinds of things you would find there, for better and for worse

# Pulp Resources

  • Operating Systems: Implementation and Designopen in new window learned from the first edition of this book. the author famously fueded with Linus Torvalds on the USENET mailing list forums of the mid 90's on how to best structure an operating system
  • Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approachopen in new window Norvig's (Google's one-time head of AI) overview of artificial intelligence
  • Game Engine Black Book: Doomopen in new window a fun discussion of the Doom source (which is open source!) and issues met porting it to various platforms in the 90s
  • The Logician and the Engineer: How George Boole and Claude Shannon Created the Information Ageopen in new window (Paul J. Nahin) Boolean logic, the 0/1 of the digital world, and the communications theory that give us the Internet and everything
  • The Discrete Charm of the Machineopen in new window (Ken Steiglitz) How the analog "real world" can entirely become the digital domain, except when it can't
  • Godel, Escher, Bach: An Eternal Golden Braidopen in new window a fantastic philosophic meander from fractals and self-reference to the limits of formal systems
  • Metaphors We Live Byopen in new window linguistics moreso than programming, but if you're playing at language AI, its breakdown of the structure of language as metaphor is great
  • Reflections on Trusting Trustopen in new window (PDF) ken thompson's famous essay
  • Worse is Betteropen in new window absolutely classic essay on how to make good software through early release and frequenty iteration
